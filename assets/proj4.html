Bee Tagging Robot

  1. Home
  2. Portfoio Details

In ongoing project for my masters in electrical engineering, I am developing a vision-based approach to predict the future position of bees for automatic tagging using a robotic delta arm while minimizing interference in their natural behavior and ensuring their safety.

The algorithm developed for this project effectively estimates the position and velocity of bees in real-time using a camera that observes their movement through a tube. The algorithm also takes into account the inherent limitations of the delta robot, such as its inability to make large corrections or instantaneous movements, and the need to maintain a safe distance from the bees to avoid disturbing them. The delta robot employs a smooth motion control strategy that allows it to make small, continuous adjustments while aiming for precise tag placement. To ensure the safety and well-being of the bees, a significant penalty has been introduced for incorrect tag positioning. The results obtained from the project demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in enabling effective positioning of a delta robot for accurate bee tagging with minimal interference in their natural motion. This will facilitate more effective bee research and conservation efforts. The successful completion of this project marks a significant milestone in the field of bee research and conservation efforts. The developed algorithm and approach can be further refined and applied to various other areas that require precision motion control with minimal impact on natural behavior.
